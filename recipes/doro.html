Doro Wat

A traditional Ethiopian Chicken Stew (Doro Wat)

Doro Wat is one of the most famous African dishes known to Ethiopia. It has been regarded as the national dish of the country.

It is a spicy chicken stew made up of caramelized onions, chicken, boiled eggs, and ground spices. This dish is traditionally eaten with a side of injera – Ethiopia’s popular fermented flatbread.

Ingredients Required to Make Doro Wat

  • Berbere & other Spices (A mix of dry chili peppers, coriander, ginger, garlic, besobela (Ethiopian holy basil seeds), rue, radhuni, fenugreek, korarima, and nigella)
  • Red Onions
  • Soft Chicken
  • Niter Kibbeh (Ethiopian clarified butter)
  • Hard-boiled Eggs
